Miso Mushroom Soup

Light, savory, and ready in ~35 minutes • serves 2

Ingredients

  • 6 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 3 cups cold water
  • ½ cup fresh spinach leaves
  • ½ cup medium silken tofu, cubed (or firm if you prefer)
  • 2½ tbsp dark miso (red or brown)
  • 2 tbsp scallion tops, sliced
  • ½ tsp toasted sesame oil (for finishing)
  • Optional: udon or other noodles

Serves 2. Easily doubled or tripled.

Directions

  1. Rehydrate mushrooms. Combine dried shiitake and 3 cups cold water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat. Remove mushrooms, discard stems, slice caps thinly.
  2. Simmer. Return sliced mushrooms to the pot, bring to a gentle simmer and cook 15–20 minutes.
  3. Add greens & tofu. Stir in spinach and silken tofu; simmer 1–2 minutes until spinach wilts and tofu is warmed through.
  4. Mix miso. In a small bowl, dissolve the miso in 2 tablespoons of hot broth from the pot until smooth.
  5. Finish soup. Remove pot from heat, stir the dissolved miso back into the soup (do not boil after adding miso). Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. Serve. Divide into bowls, sprinkle with sliced scallions and drizzle ¼ tsp toasted sesame oil per serving.

Notes & variations

  • Protein: Omit tofu and add cooked shredded chicken or thinly sliced beef if you prefer.
  • Texture: Use medium silken tofu for a silky texture; firm tofu works for chunks.
  • Tip: Always dissolve miso in hot (not boiling) broth and stir in off heat to keep delicate flavors.
