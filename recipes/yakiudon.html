Yaki Udon — Stir-Fried Udon Noodles

Easy Japanese weeknight dinner • serves 2–3

Ingredients

  • 2 packs (12–14 oz) udon noodles (thawed/loosened)
  • 8–10 oz protein: shrimp, thin-sliced chicken, pork, or tofu
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage (or bok choy)
  • 1 medium carrot, sliced
  • 4–5 mushrooms, sliced (optional)
  • 2 green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Sauce

  • 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin (or rice wine)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • Optional: pinch gochugaru (for heat)

Prep tip: If using frozen udon, loosen by soaking in hot water 30–60 seconds, drain, and rinse under cold water; set aside.

Directions

  1. Prep sauce. Whisk soy sauce, oyster sauce, mirin, and brown sugar; keep nearby.
  2. Cook protein. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Stir-fry protein until mostly cooked; remove to plate.
  3. Sauté aromatics & veg. Add onion and garlic 30–45s, add carrots/cabbage/mushrooms and stir-fry 1–2 min.
  4. Add noodles. Add udon, toss and heat through; splash 1–2 tbsp water if clumping.
  5. Combine & finish. Return protein, pour sauce, toss 1–2 min until glossy. Taste and adjust.
  6. Serve. Garnish with green onions, sesame seeds, kewpie mayo or bonito flakes if desired.

Tips & variations

  • Vegetable swaps: Bell pepper, bok choy, spinach, or leftover veggies work.
  • Protein ideas: Bacon, thin-sliced beef, or tofu.
  • Char flavor: Cook on high heat and avoid overcrowding for slight charring.
  • Storage: Keeps 2–3 days; reheat in a pan with a splash of water.
