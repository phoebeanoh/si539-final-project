Cold Soba

3 ingredients • ready in 5-10 minutes

Ingredients

  • Soba noodles (or any thin Asian noodles—soba tastes best!)
  • ½ cup soba dipping sauce (store-bought, Way1 Asian Mart has great options)
  • ½ tsp wasabi powder (optional but adds a nice kick!)

Optional toppings: sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, shredded nori, or a squeeze of lime.

Directions

  1. Cook the noodles. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add soba and cook according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop cooking and remove starch.
  2. Make the sauce. In a small bowl, stir together the dipping sauce and wasabi powder until smooth.
  3. Dip & enjoy. Serve noodles cold with a bowl of sauce on the side. Dip each bite and slurp!

Tips & Tricks

  • Rinse soba well. This removes extra starch so the noodles stay loose and silky, not gummy.
  • Chill the noodles. For peak refreshing flavor, rinse with ice water or refrigerate for 5–10 minutes before serving.
  • Add proteins. Great additions: boiled eggs, tofu, or sliced cucumbers for crunch.
  • Upgrade the sauce. Add a splash of rice vinegar or sesame oil if you like more depth.
  • Meal prep. Keep noodles and sauce separate — mix only when eating so the soba doesn't soften.
  • Don’t overcook. Soba goes mushy fast; start checking 30–60 seconds before the package suggests.
