Dango (Chewy Japanese Rice Dumplings)

2-ingredient dough • chewy + lightly sweet • makes 20–25 dango

Ingredients

For dango

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour (shiratamako or mochiko)
  • ~½ cup warm water (add slowly until dough comes together)
  • Bowl of cold water (for cooling)
  • Wooden skewers, pre-soaked

Mitarashi topping

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp water
  • ½–1 tsp cornstarch (to thicken)

Tip: Use Erawan green-label glutinous rice flour if Japanese brands are unavailable.

Directions

  1. Make the dough. Mix flour and warm water a little at a time until smooth and soft like an earlobe.
  2. Shape the dango. Roll into ~2 cm/1-inch balls.
  3. Boil. Drop into boiling water; when they float, cook 1 more minute.
  4. Cool. Transfer to cold water to firm slightly.
  5. Skewer. Thread 3–4 dango per skewer. Optional: pan-sear until lightly toasted.
  6. Top. Spoon mitarashi glaze or anko over skewers and serve warm.

How to make the toppings

Mitarashi glaze

Whisk soy sauce, sugar, water, and cornstarch; heat and stir until thick and glossy. Spoon over warm dango.

Anko topping

Spoon sweet red bean paste over skewered dango; warm slightly for best texture.

Quick Tips & Storage

  • If dough cracks, add 1–2 tsp water; if sticky, dust hands with rice flour.
  • Dango is best the same day; to re-soften refrigerated dango, steam or microwave briefly.
