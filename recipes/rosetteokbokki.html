Rosé tteokbokki (로제 떡볶이)

Creamy, slightly spicy rice cakes • serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 lb rice cakes for tteokbokki (soak if refrigerated)
  • 4 strips bacon, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 8 mini sausages, slits cut into each
  • ¼ medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1 green onion, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, peeled (optional)

For the sauce

  • 2 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp gochu-garu (Korean chili powder)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup water (plus 1 cup extra for cooking)

Directions

  1. Mix the sauce. In a bowl whisk gochujang, gochu-garu, soy sauce, sugar, milk, cream, and ½ cup water until smooth.
  2. Prepare rice cakes. Drain soaked rice cakes and set aside.
  3. Cook bacon. Cook bacon in a thick-bottomed pan over medium heat until crispy; drain excess fat.
  4. Sauté aromatics & sausage. Add garlic and onion; stir 1 minute. Add sausages and rice cakes and stir briefly.
  5. Simmer rice cakes. Add 1 cup water, cover, and simmer 4–6 minutes until soft.
  6. Add sauce & cheese. Pour in sauce, cook 4–5 minutes, add mozzarella and stir until melted.
  7. Finish & serve. Garnish with green onion and halved eggs. Serve hot.

Tips & Tricks!

  • Texture: If sauce is too thick, add a splash of water or milk.
  • Bacon/sausage: Use vegetarian alternatives if preferred (e.g., mushrooms).
  • Make-ahead: Sauce can be refrigerated up to 24 hours.
