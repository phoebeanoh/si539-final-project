Peanut Butter Noodles

Yummy, creamy noodles! Amazing for peanut butter fans! • serves 4–6

Ingredients

  • Salt, for the pasta water
  • 1 lb long noodles (spaghetti, linguine, udon, or ramen)
  • ⅓ cup creamy no-stir peanut butter
  • ¼ cup sweet chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (more to taste)
  • ¼ cup boiling water (from the pasta pot), plus more as needed
  • Vegetables of your choice (e.g., brussels sprouts, sautéed onions)

Optional toppings: sliced green onions, chopped cilantro, toasted sesame seeds, crushed peanuts, or a squeeze of lime.

Directions

  1. Cook the noodles. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook noodles to al dente per package instructions. Drain and rinse under cool water until no longer hot (this helps the sauce cling and stops cooking).
  2. Make the sauce. In a large mixing bowl whisk together peanut butter, sweet chili sauce, soy sauce, and ¼ cup boiling water from the pot. Whisk vigorously until silky and pourable. Add more water, a tablespoon at a time, if you want a thinner sauce.
  3. Sauté the veggies. Cook up the veggies in a separate pan. Add to noodles after tossing in sauce.
  4. Toss & finish. Add the drained, cooled noodles to the sauce. Use tongs to toss until evenly coated. Add veggies and mix. Taste and adjust with more soy or chili sauce.
  5. Serve. Divide among bowls and top with green onions, cilantro, sesame seeds, or crushed peanuts. Serve immediately.

Notes & storage

  • Noodle options: Thick spaghetti or udon give the best bite; pad Thai rice noodles or soba both work.
  • Make-ahead: Sauce can be prepared in advance. Toss noodles just before serving. Leftovers keep 3 days refrigerated; add a splash of hot water when reheating to loosen the sauce.
  • Spice: Add sriracha, gochujang, or crushed red pepper to taste.
